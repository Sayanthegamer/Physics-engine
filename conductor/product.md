# Product Definition: GearEngine

## Vision Statement
A performance-focused, standalone 3D mechanical physics engine built in C++20. It uses a strict Data-Oriented Design (DOD) paradigm to provide high-fidelity, deterministic mechanical simulations (such as gears, cogs, and automated wire routing) while maintaining smooth performance on budget hardware and offering a premium CAD-style 3D interactive interface.

## Target Audience
The engine is designed for a vast and diverse audience:
- **Game Developers & Gamers** seeking a highly optimized engine for creative and specialized mechanics, or just having fun experimenting.
- **Engineers & Hobbyists** building complex mechanical systems (e.g., full combustion engines) from primitive parts.
- **Non-Technical Users** who want an accessible, premium interactive experience for experimentation.

## Current Milestones & Goals
The immediate short-term goals focus on core engine capabilities rather than finalizing the UI:
- Finalizing internal rendering and logic.
- Implementing robust constraint solvers.
- Developing diverse gear types and making them fully 3D.
- Building the foundational toolbars and CAD-style interaction mechanisms.

## Performance Targets
- **Extreme Efficiency:** The engine must run smoothly on low-end budget hardware, specifically targeting an Intel i3 10th Gen U-series CPU running on its integrated graphics.
